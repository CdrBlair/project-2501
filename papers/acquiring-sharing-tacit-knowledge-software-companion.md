**Key Contribution:**

## 3. Key Findings

| Finding | Evidence Strength | Confidence |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| Effective social interactions crucial for acquiring/sharing tacit knowledge | Strong | High |
| Quality of interactions more significant than TMS alone | Strong | High |
| Both TMS and team tacit knowledge predict team effectiveness | Strong | High |
| TMS and tacit knowledge do NOT predict team efficiency | Strong | High |
| Collocated, functionally diverse teams enhance tacit knowledge sharing | Moderate | Medium-High |
| Face-to-face interactions key to effective tacit knowledge sharing | Strong | High |

**Key Research Questions Addressed:**

1. **How do software development teams acquire and share tacit knowledge?**
   - Through effective social interactions
   - Face-to-face communication particularly important
   - Functional diversity supports knowledge acquisition

2. **What roles do tacit knowledge and transactive memory play in team performance?**
   - Both predict team *effectiveness*
   - Neither predicts team *efficiency* directly
   - Quality of interactions mediates the relationship

## 5. Practical Implications

**For Team Organisation:**

1. **Collocated teams** recommended for tacit knowledge sharing
2. **Functionally diverse** teams enhance knowledge acquisition
3. **Face-to-face interaction** should be prioritised
4. Social interaction quality more important than formal knowledge systems

**For Agile Methods:**

- Provides empirical support for agile's emphasis on:
  - Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  - Working software over comprehensive documentation
  - Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  - Responding to change over following a plan

**For AI Integration:**

- AI cannot substitute for social interaction in tacit knowledge transfer
- Human-to-human interaction remains essential
- AI may support but not replace face-to-face knowledge sharing
- Risk: Over-reliance on AI may reduce valuable social interactions

## 6. Limitations

### Author-Acknowledged Limitations

- Single organisation or limited sample (typical of empirical SE studies)
- Self-report measures for some constructs
- Cross-sectional design (no longitudinal dynamics)

### Additional Limitations

- **Generalisability**: Findings may not apply to all software contexts
- **Remote teams**: Study predates widespread remote work; findings about collocation may need updating
- **AI context**: Pre-AI era study; how AI affects dynamics not addressed
- **Efficiency vs. effectiveness**: Distinction important but mechanisms unclear
